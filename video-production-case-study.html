GIT 315: Digital Video Techniques

Multi-Track Post-Production Sequence | Graphic Information Technology at Arizona State University

🎯 Production Backstory & Academic Scope

This case study features the progressive, three-part production sequence completed for **GIT 315: Digital Video Techniques** at **Arizona State University**. The curriculum focused heavily on technical, non-linear editing mechanics, rigorous pre-production planning workflows, and multi-track pacing utilizing Adobe Premiere Pro ecosystems.

Every project in the sequence strictly enforced advanced production criteria: utilizing 100% original, self-shot video layers (no stock media, outside video captures, or video game clips allowed), engineering continuous multi-shot timeline tracks to break up long single clips, and executing tight pacing constraints to lock project runtimes perfectly within narrow academic execution parameters.

🎬 Progressive Production Sequence & Course Deliverables

The technical roadmap executed across consecutive post-production tracks to meet strict Ira A. Fulton Schools of Engineering criteria:

  • 📦 Assignment 2: "Favorite Video" (mooreAssignment2)

    Prompt Constraints: Engineered a 1-to-2 minute profile focused entirely on original, self-shot mobile captures. Developed a rich multi-shot layout utilizing Premiere Pro's cut tools to replace static, single-camera takes with active visual movement, anchoring the narrative to a synchronized, clear voiceover track.

  • 📦 Assignment 4: "Personal Video" Sequence

    Technical Workflows: Built a story-driven digital narrative completely isolated from external unboxing media or screen captures. Managed project scaling through strict export pipeline configurations, encoding master streams with an H.264 codec template at a crisp YouTube 1080p HD (or Vimeo 1080p) target container specification.

  • 🚀 Final Project: Original 30-Second Commercial

    Pre-Production & Engineering: Directed pre-production pipelines by authoring clean project structural outlines and a professional two-column AV (Audio/Video) script matrix, matching industry-standard abbreviations (e.g., MS for Medium Shot) to audio timelines before shooting original product concept loops.

⚡ Technical Post-Production Implementations

  • Multi-Shot Layering: Dissected raw continuous camera frames into deliberate multi-shot sequence clips, applying framing mechanics studied across the GIT curriculum.
  • Asynchronous Voiceover Syncing: Combined on-screen appearances and crisp, external mic voiceover tracks inside the Premiere Pro timeline, balancing ambient audio tracks manually to maintain vocal clarity.
  • Non-Linear Asset Management: Managed complete source directory pipelines for raw components, enforcing tidy file structures to optimize rendering performance speeds during final export cycles.
